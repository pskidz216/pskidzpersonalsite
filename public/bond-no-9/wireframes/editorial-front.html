Annotations · Option A

What the page is doing.

  1. Hero is one cinematic frame. Full-bleed campaign film of NYC, not a packshot. Single piece of brand copy — "An address you live at." — pulled from Bond's belief statement. One understated CTA. The bottle is not visible above the fold; the city is.
  2. Neighborhood of the moment — Le Labo's "City Exclusive" mechanic, adapted. Rotates monthly: Madison Avenue this month, Bleecker next, Brooklyn after. Each gets photography, a short editorial story, and a shop-the-neighborhood CTA. This is where the brand's strongest asset (NYC neighborhoods) earns homepage real estate.
  3. Perfumer voice — Laurice Rahmé. Founder/perfumer block earns the price point. MFK move. "Twenty-three years independent" is one of Bond's strongest reasons-to-believe — surfaced here, not buried.
  4. Discovery surfaces at scroll 4. Two doors only: quiz and Discovery Set ($45 / $40 credited). The quiz is now framed as "Find your neighborhood" — language pulled directly from the brand word.
  5. Featured scents are three editorial cards. Each named by neighborhood (Bleecker, Madison Square, Brooklyn) — never a 12-tile "shop now" carousel. Bond's rule: "If it could appear on Amazon, it cannot appear on Bond No. 9."
  6. Boutique block is a major homepage section. 9 Bond Street is a brand asset; treating it as a footer link wastes it. Editorial photo of the storefront + "Book an appointment" CTA. "The boutique footprint" is one of Bond's five reasons-to-believe.
  7. Press logos understated. Editorial Front trades social proof density for restraint. Outlets chosen are the ones the Native Cosmopolitan actually reads, per the audience media diet: Vogue, NYT Styles, Air Mail, T Magazine, Harper's, Vanity Fair.
  8. Newsletter framed as "A letter from NoHo." Brand-aligned voice — never "subscribe to our emails." Treats the customer as in on the joke, per Bond's personality rules.
