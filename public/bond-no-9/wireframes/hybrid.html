Annotations · Option C

What the page is doing.

  1. Hero is one cinematic frame — same as Option A. Full-bleed film of NYC. The brand statement "An address you live at." pulled directly from Bond's belief statement. One CTA — "↓ Continue" — gestures to the scroll, not to a transaction. The visitor has not been asked to make a choice yet.
  2. The fold breaks here intentionally. Hero earns the scroll; the scroll rewards with the actual decision. Per Bond's editorial principles: "we do not explain ourselves to anyone who is not already curious."
  3. Scroll 1 is Two Doors. Now the visitor has oriented and is ready to commit to a path. The split happens HERE — not at the threshold, where it would bounce someone who hadn't yet understood the brand. Door 02 ("Find your neighborhood") leads with the brand word, not "take a quiz."
  4. Scroll 2 is the Discovery trio. Quiz · Discovery Set · Wardrobe — three doors. Discovery Set is the centerpiece (gold-bordered, surface-filled) with the $45 / $40 credited mechanic surfaced. Bond's "accessibility at $295, generosity at $470" pricing logic extends here: $45 is the accessible entry to the wardrobe.
  5. Scrolls 3–4 are merchandising + social proof. "Currently wearing" replaces "Bestsellers" — voice-aligned with Bond's register. Social proof leads with a pulled-quote from the audience profile ("What are you wearing? It's perfect on you.") + named-customer endorsement.
  6. Scrolls 5–7 are brand-world — same content as Option A's scrolls 2–6, just relocated to the back half. Neighborhood feature, perfumer voice, boutique. They deepen the impression instead of competing for the conversion mechanic's real estate.
  7. Net structure: brand-world dominates the impression (hero + scrolls 5–7), discovery dominates the action zone (scrolls 1–2). The two arguments stop competing for the same real estate. Both win.
  8. One execution dependency: Option C only works if the hero film earns the scroll. There's no transactional CTA above the fold to bail out a weak frame. The standard to clear: the hero has to make the visitor want to see what's below it. Treat the hero shoot brief like a fashion campaign, not a product film.
