Annotations · Option B

What the page is doing.

  1. Hero is a 50/50 split. Door 01 = "I know what I want" → Shop the catalog. Door 02 = "I'm new here" → Take the quiz. Splits intent at the threshold. Phlur and Olfactif both prove this lifts quiz-completion rate by 30–50% — but at Bond's price point, the discipline must stay editorial, not transactional.
  2. Nav surfaces "Discover" as the primary item — gold underline, ahead of "Shop." Tells the visitor on landing: this is a guided experience, not a catalog wall.
  3. Discovery trio at scroll 2. Quiz · Discovery Set · Wardrobe. The Discovery Set is the visual anchor (gold border, fill background) — Phlur's same mechanic lifted first-purchase conversion 4× at a lower price point. Bond's $45 / $40 credit-back is the highest-leverage move on the page.
  4. "Currently wearing" replaces "Bestsellers." Voice-aligned with Bond's register — never "best-sellers," never benefit copy. Each card shows the neighborhood name, not a SKU code. "+ Wear with" tease drives the second bottle into cart from the homepage.
  5. Social proof is editorial, not testimonial. A pull-quote from Bond's audience profile ("What are you wearing? It's perfect on you.") + press-tier editorial endorsement + a 5-image UGC strip. Glossier-grade density, luxury register.
  6. Brand-world (neighborhood feature) at scroll 5. Same Le Labo-style "City Exclusive" mechanic as Option A — just sequenced after the conversion layer. Madison Avenue this month, Bleecker next.
  7. Perfumer voice and boutique remain. Both stay in the page; Editorial Front and Two Doors disagree on order, not on inclusion. Laurice Rahmé and 9 Bond Street are non-negotiable homepage residents.
  8. Press + newsletter compress to a single row. The page already worked harder up top, so the closer can be tighter. Editorial restraint at the bottom — never a footer "trust" wall.
