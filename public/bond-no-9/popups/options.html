A note before you read

This is guidance — not gospel.

Three options follow. Pick one, mix two, throw all of it out and write your own. The point isn't which popup ships. The point is to do two things:

  • ·Collect the data. Email + SMS, opted in at a Bond-appropriate rate, with measurable lift in Revenue Per Session vs. non-opted-in traffic. That's the only number that matters.
  • ·Look different. Every other niche fragrance house runs the same "Sign up for 15% off → no wait, 15% off later → here's the spin wheel" flow. The popup is the first thing the visitor sees — make it the first place Bond reads like Bond and nobody else.

Whichever variant gets you there is the right one. Take what's useful, leave the rest.

Three brand-safe popups, each paired to a homepage option.

04.C / Option C · 2-Step Cashback
2-step cashback · Fondue mechanic · highest opt-in · most conservative

The cashback move — discount without the discount voice.

Pairs with Homepage B · Two Doors

2-step popup using cashback (via Fondue) instead of upfront percent-off. $25 cashback — no code, no minimum, no price tag reduction — sent as a Visa gift card or store credit after the order ships. On Bond's register because price label and product page never show a discount. Highest opt-in (industry: 25–32%). No SMS. Pairs with Homepage B because both options optimize for fast comprehension and direct conversion paths.

Why this works

  • Highest opt-in rate (industry: 25–32% — beats 3-step and quiz formats)

  • Lowest build complexity — Fondue + Attentive, no quiz logic

Trade-offs

  • No phone capture in this popup — must be captured elsewhere
  • Less brand-distinctive than A or B — known DTC pattern even if luxury-coherent
  • Doesn't connect to the quiz or to neighborhood thesis — transactional, not discovery
04.X / Anti-patterns · never ship

Six things any of the three options must never do.

Cross-cutting brand and CRO discipline that applies to all three popup options. These are the common popup failures that turn a 21% opt-in into a brand liability.

✕ Page-load popup Doubles bounce rate. Bond removed this once already — do not bring it back. Trigger must be exit-intent / scroll / time-delay (≥7s).
✕ % off discount in copy Off register. "10% off" / "15% off" / "Save 20%" all forbidden. Substitute: cashback (C), exclusivity (A), discovery (B).
✕ Email + phone same step Kills opt-in. Option A separates email (Step 2) from phone (Step 3). C drops phone entirely.
✕ Hidden / unclosable X Always bad UX. X visible top-right of every step at minimum 14×14px hit target on desktop, 24×24px on mobile.
✕ Stacked popups in one session One popup per session, period. No newsletter + SMS + country + age-gate cascade.
✕ "Save for Later" final CTA Use "Continue browsing" instead. "Save for Later" reads as the user is leaving — wrong signal at opt-in moment.
Pairing summary · which popup with which homepage
  • — Option A · 3-Step Perfect (Bond) → Homepage A · Editorial Front. The luxury-magazine pairing. Both bet on long-form brand-world reading time and editorial register over fast conversion.
  • — Option B · Question-Based Quiz → Homepage C · The Hybrid. The brand-defining pairing — recommended pick. Both options earn their weight through the discovery quiz mechanic. Same mechanic, two surfaces.
  • — Option C · 2-Step Cashback → Homepage B · Two Doors. The pragmatic conversion-velocity pairing. Both optimize for fast comprehension and direct conversion paths. Lowest joint build cost.
Cross-cuts · all three options
  • — Trigger: Exit-intent OR 50% scroll · NEVER load-immediate. Option B may use 10s delay as a third trigger.
  • — Frequency cap: 1 per 7 days (14 for Option B). New visitors only by default.
  • — Suppression: No popup on /cart, /checkout, /account. Fires on home, collection, neighborhoods, story pages only.
  • — Brand voice: editorial, NYC-native, no exclamations, no urgency, no sale-theater language. Bond Gold for accents only (Option C exception: gold CTA because gold IS the offer).
  • — Confirmation: "Continue browsing" CTA, never "Save for Later." Cookie set immediately. Routes vary by option.
  • — Attentive Multi-Step Creative, not Shopify-native. Native form lacks exit-intent and multi-step. Attentive handles SMS natively (two-tap TCPA opt-in) — no separate Postscript needed.
